I am a first-year PhD Student in Meteorology and Physical Oceanography at the University of Miami Rosenstiel School of Marine, Atmospheric, and Earth Science. I am advised by Dr. Amy Clement, and my graduate research focuses on the evolving role of the ocean and atmosphere on global climate variability in climate models.

I graduated from the Pennsylvania State University in Spring 2025, where I received a B.S. in Meteorology and Atmospheric Science with Honors, along with minors in Marine Science and Geography, and an undergraduate certificate in Geographic Information Science (GIS). During undergrad, I participated in multiple research experiences, studying biogeochemistry and carbon capture implementation in the Chespeake Bay through the Penn State Meteorology Department, studying the carbon and energy budgets of a forest through an NSF REU program at Plymouth State University, and interning with the NOAA Physical Sciences Laboratory through the NOAA Hollings Program where I studied the impact of ENSO on the California Current System.

In my free time, I can be found reading, painting, spending time outdoors, and enjoying live music.
